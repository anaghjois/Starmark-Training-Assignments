Jog Falls

Jog Falls is created by the Sharavati dropping 253 m (830 ft), making it the third-highest waterfall in India after the Nohkalikai Falls with a drop of 335 m (1,099 ft) in Meghalaya and the Dudhsagar Falls with a drop of 310 m (1,020 ft) in Goa.

Linganamakki Dam

The Linganamakki Dam was constructed by the Karnataka State Government in 1964. Located in the Kargal village of Sagara taluk, the dam has a length of 2.74 kilometres stretching across the Sharavathi river. It is located about 9 km from Jog Falls. It has a storage capacity of 4.29 cubic kms or 151.52 tmc ft of water.

Dabbe Falls

Dabbe Falls is a waterfall located in place named Dabbe near Hosagadde in Sagar taluk, Shivamogga District in Karnataka state, India. The height of the falls is 110 meters. One can take a guide from Muppane nature camp which is 10 km away from Dabbe Falls.
